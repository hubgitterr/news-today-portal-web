Allergy season is arriving sooner!

Scientists say allergy season is starting sooner and lasting longer because of climate change. Allergists said at one time, pollen season didn’t kick off until St. Patrick’s Day. Now higher pollen counts can start as early as Valentine's Day. According to a study from the journal Me and Nature, as the world becomes warmer, allergy season will start weeks sooner and end many days later. Springtime in general gives some people job stability. Once mid-March hits, Joe Wright said this is a busy time of year for landscaping. Climate scientists said a warmer world directly ties to an earlier and longer allergy season. In another study published in the Journal of Sciences in 2020, human-caused climate change could impact respiratory health because of higher concentrations of airborne pollen. Wright said he has been in a landscaping job for 10 years. He said no matter the impact on his own health, for him it will be business as usual like this time every March.
